♦ Classical and Conditional Probability

Problem 1: Basic Probability

A standard deck of 52 cards is shuffled. What is the probability of drawing a red card?

Red cards: Hearts (13) + Diamonds (13) = 26 cards
Total cards: 52
P(Red) = Number of red cards / Total number of cards = 26/52 = 1/2 = 0.5

Problem 2: Conditional Probability

Given that a card drawn is red, what is the probability it's a heart?

We know the card is red (26 possibilities)
Hearts among red cards: 13
P(Heart | Red) = P(Heart ∩ Red) / P(Red)
P(Heart | Red) = 13/26 = 1/2 = 0.5 (since half of the red cards are hearts)

Problem 3: Bayes' Theorem

A medical test is 95% accurate for a disease that affects 1% of the population. If someone tests positive, what's the probability they have the disease?

P(Disease) = 0.01
P(Test+ | Disease) = 0.95
P(Test+ | No Disease) = 0.05
Find: P(Disease | Test+)
Use Bayes: P(D|T+) = P(T+|D)·P(D) / [P(T+|D)·P(D) + P(T+|¬D)·P(¬D)] = (0.95·0.01) / [0.95·0.01 + 0.05·0.99]

Problem 4: Independence

Two fair dice are rolled. What is the probability that both show even numbers?

Even numbers on a die: 2, 4, 6 (3 out of 6)
Events are independent
P(Both even) = P(First even) × P(Second even)
P(Even) = 3/6 = 1/2 for each die. P(Both even) = 1/2 × 1/2 = 1/4 = 0.25

Problem 5: Law of Total Probability

A factory has 3 machines. Machine A produces 50% (2% defective), Machine B produces 30% (3% defective), Machine C produces 20% (5% defective). What's the overall defect rate?

P(A) = 0.5, P(Defect|A) = 0.02
P(B) = 0.3, P(Defect|B) = 0.03
P(C) = 0.2, P(Defect|C) = 0.05
P(Defect) = P(A)·P(D|A) + P(B)·P(D|B) + P(C)·P(D|C) = 0.5·0.02 + 0.3·0.03 + 0.2·0.05 = 0.029
