Problem 1: Circle in Square

A circle with radius 3 is inscribed in a square. A point is randomly chosen in the square. What's the probability it lands in the circle?

┌─────────┐
│ ○○○ │
│ ○○○○○ │
│ ○○○●○○○ │ ← Square side = 6
│ ○○○○○ │
│ ○○○ │
└─────────┘
Circle radius = 3, so area = π × 3² = 9π
Square side = 6, so area = 6² = 36
P = Circle area / Square area
P = (9π)/36 = π/4 ≈ 0.785

Problem 2: Dartboard Probability

A dartboard has concentric circles with radii 2, 4, and 6. What's the probability of hitting the middle ring (between radii 2 and 4)?

●●●●●●●
●●○○○○○●●
●●○○░○○○●●
●●○○░○○○●● ← Outer ring
●●○○○○○○●● ← Middle ring
●●○○○○○●● ← Inner circle
●●●●●●●
Total area = π × 6² = 36π
Middle ring area = π × 4² - π × 2² = 16π - 4π = 12π
P = 12π/36π = 12/36 = 1/3 ≈ 0.333

Problem 3: Buffon's Needle Problem (Simplified)

Parallel lines are 4 units apart. A needle of length 2 is dropped randomly. What's the probability it crosses a line?

Line spacing = 4 units
Needle length = 2 units
Classical result: P = (2 × length) / (π × spacing)
P = (2 × 2)/(π × 4) = 4/(4π) = 1/π ≈ 0.318

Problem 5: Meeting Time Problem

Two people agree to meet between 2:00 and 3:00 PM. Each arrives at a random time and waits 15 minutes. What's the probability they meet?

Let x = arrival time of person 1 (0 to 60 minutes)
Let y = arrival time of person 2 (0 to 60 minutes)
They meet if |x - y| ≤ 15
Total area = 60² = 3600. Meeting region area = 3600 - 2×(45²/2) = 3600 - 2025 = 1575. P = 1575/3600 ≈ 0.44
