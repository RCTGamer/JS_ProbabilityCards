Drawing Cards Until Success

A standard deck of cards is shuffled. On average, how many cards will you have to draw to get the A♣ if:

a) You draw with replacement?
b) You draw without replacement?
a)
b)
Hint ▼

Part a): With replacement, this is a geometric distribution. The expected number of trials until the first success is 1/p.

Part b): Without replacement, think about the expected position of the A♣ in a shuffled deck.

Solution ▼
Part a) With replacement:
Each draw has probability $p = \frac{1}{52}$ of getting A♣.
This follows a geometric distribution with expected value $\frac{1}{p} = \frac{1}{1/52} = 52$
Part b) Without replacement:
The A♣ is equally likely to be in any of the 52 positions in the shuffled deck.
Expected position = $\frac{1 + 2 + 3 + ... + 52}{52} = \frac{52 \times 53}{2 \times 52} = \frac{53}{2} = 26.5$
