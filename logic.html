Problem 1: Number Pattern

Find the missing number in this sequence:

2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ?, 56
Look at the differences: 6-2=4, 12-6=6, 20-12=8, 30-20=10. The pattern is +4, +6, +8, +10, +12...

Problem 3: Weighing Puzzle

You have 8 balls that look identical. Seven weigh the same, but one is heavier. Using a balance scale only twice, how do you find the heavy ball?

Step 1: Divide the 8 balls into three groups. What should the group sizes be?
Try dividing into groups of 3, 3, and 2. Weigh the two groups of 3 against each other first.

Problem 4: Time Logic

If it takes 5 machines 5 minutes to make 5 widgets, how long would it take 100 machines to make 100 widgets?

If 5 machines make 5 widgets in 5 minutes, each machine makes 1 widget in 5 minutes. So 100 machines make 100 widgets in how long?

Problem 5: Age Puzzle

Sarah is twice as old as her brother Tom.
In 6 years, Sarah will be 1.5 times as old as Tom.
How old is Sarah now?
Let Tom's age = x. Then Sarah's age = 2x. In 6 years: Sarah = 2x+6, Tom = x+6. And 2x+6 = 1.5(x+6).

Problem 6: River Crossing

A farmer needs to cross a river with a fox, chicken, and bag of corn. The boat can only carry the farmer plus one item. The fox cannot be left alone with the chicken, and the chicken cannot be left alone with the corn.

What should the farmer take across first?
Think about which item, if left alone, would cause problems with the remaining two items.
