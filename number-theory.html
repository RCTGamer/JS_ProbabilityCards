Problem 1: Prime Numbers

How many prime numbers are there between 1 and 20?

Prime numbers are natural numbers greater than 1 that have no positive divisors other than 1 and themselves.

The prime numbers between 1 and 20 are: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19. Count them!

Problem 3: Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)

Find the GCD of 48 and 18.

The GCD is the largest positive integer that divides both numbers.

Use the Euclidean algorithm: 48 = 18 √ó 2 + 12, 18 = 12 √ó 1 + 6, 12 = 6 √ó 2 + 0. GCD = 6.

Problem 5: Perfect Numbers

What is the smallest perfect number?

A perfect number is a positive integer that is equal to the sum of its proper positive divisors.

Think of a small number whose divisors (excluding itself) add up to the number. Try 6: divisors are 1, 2, 3. Does 1+2+3 = 6?
