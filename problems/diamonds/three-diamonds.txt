Conditional Dealing

We shuffle a deck of cards and I deal you the **3♦, 4♦, and 5♦**.
I look at the next card and tell you that it is between 3 and 8 (inclusive).
How likely is it that this card is a ♦?

[HINTS]
Start by considering which cards remain in the deck after dealing you the three diamonds.

Then determine how many cards satisfy the condition of being between 3 and 8 inclusive.

Finally, calculate what portion of those cards are diamonds.

[SOLUTION]
After dealing the 3♦, 4♦, and 5♦, there are 49 cards left in the deck.

Among these 49 cards, there are 10 diamonds left (13 original diamonds minus the 3 that were dealt).

The condition is that the next card is between 3 and 8 inclusive. This means the card's rank could be 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, or 8.

For each rank, there are 4 cards in the deck (one of each suit), but some have already been dealt:
- Rank 3: 3♣, 3♥, 3♠ (3♦ was dealt)
- Rank 4: 4♣, 4♥, 4♠ (4♦ was dealt)
- Rank 5: 5♣, 5♥, 5♠ (5♦ was dealt)
- Rank 6: 6♣, 6♥, 6♠, 6♦
- Rank 7: 7♣, 7♥, 7♠, 7♦
- Rank 8: 8♣, 8♥, 8♠, 8♦

So there are 3 + 3 + 3 + 4 + 4 + 4 = 21 cards that satisfy the condition of being between 3 and 8 inclusive.

Of these 21 cards, the diamonds are 6♦, 7♦, 8♦, which is 3 cards.

Therefore, the probability that the next card is a diamond given that it's between 3 and 8 inclusive is:

$P(\text{diamond} | \text{between 3 and 8}) = \frac{3}{21} = \frac{1}{7}$
